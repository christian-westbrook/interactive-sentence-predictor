==Events==
* [[49 BC]] &amp;ndash; [[Julius Caesar]]'s [[general]] [[Gaius Scribonius Curio]] is defeated in the [[Battle of the Bagradas (49 BC)]] by the [[Numidians]] under [[Publius Attius Varus]] and King [[Juba I of Numidia|Juba]] of [[Numidia]]. Curio commits suicide to avoid capture.
* [[79 AD]] &amp;ndash; [[Mount Vesuvius]] [[Eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79|erupts]]. The cities of [[Pompeii]], [[Herculaneum]], and [[Stabiae]] are buried in volcanic ash (note: this traditional date has been challenged, and many scholars believe that the event occurred on October 24).
* [[367]] &amp;ndash; [[Gratian]], son of [[Roman emperor|Roman Emperor]] [[Valentinian I]], is named co-[[Augustus (honorific)|Augustus]] at the age of eight by his father .
* [[394]] &amp;ndash; The [[Graffito of Esmet-Akhom]], the latest known inscription in [[Egyptian hieroglyphs]], was written.
* [[410]] &amp;ndash; The [[Visigoths]] under king [[Alaric I]] begin to [[Sack of Rome (410)|pillage Rome]].
* [[455]] &amp;ndash; The [[Vandals]], led by king [[Genseric]], begin to [[Sack of Rome (455)|plunder Rome]]. [[Pope Leo I]] requests Genseric not destroy the [[Ancient Rome|ancient]] city or murder its citizens. He agrees and the [[City gate|gates]] of [[Ancient Rome|Rome]] are opened. However, the Vandals loot a great amount of treasure.
*[[1185]] &amp;ndash; [[Sack of Thessalonica (1185)|Sack of Thessalonica]] by the Normans.
*[[1200]] &amp;ndash; King [[John of England]], signer of the first [[Magna Carta]], marries [[Isabella of Angoulême]] in [[Bordeaux Cathedral]].
*[[1215]] &amp;ndash; [[Pope Innocent III]] declares [[Magna Carta]] invalid.
*[[1349]] &amp;ndash; Six thousand Jews are killed in [[Mainz]] after being blamed for the [[bubonic plague]].&lt;ref name=&quot;Tuchman&quot;&gt;{{Cite book|last=Tuchman|first=Barbara Wertheim|title=A distant mirror|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BmRoOIwLWhsC&amp;pg=PT113|accessdate=27 August 2011|publisher=Random House Digital, Inc.|isbn=978-0-307-29160-8|page=116}}&lt;/ref&gt;
*[[1456]] &amp;ndash; The printing of the [[Gutenberg Bible]] is completed.
*[[1482]] &amp;ndash; The town and castle of [[capture of Berwick (1482)|Berwick upon Tweed is captured]] from Scotland by an English army
*[[1516]] &amp;ndash; The [[Ottoman Empire]] under [[Selim I]] defeats the [[Mamluk Sultanate (Cairo)|Mamluk Sultanate]] and captures present-day Syria at the [[Battle of Marj Dabiq]].
*[[1561]] &amp;ndash; [[William the Silent|Willem of Orange]] marries duchess [[Anna of Saxony]].
*[[1608]] &amp;ndash; The first official English representative to India lands in [[Surat]].
*[[1662]] &amp;ndash; The [[Act of Uniformity 1662|Act of Uniformity]] requires England to accept the [[Book of Common Prayer]].
*[[1682]] &amp;ndash; [[William Penn]] receives the area that is now the state of [[Delaware]], and adds it to his [[colony]] of [[Pennsylvania]].
*[[1690]] &amp;ndash; [[Job Charnock]] of the [[East India Company]] establishes a [[Factory (trading post)|factory]] in [[Kolkata|Calcutta]], an event formerly considered the founding of the city (in 2003 the [[Calcutta High Court]] ruled that the city's foundation date is unknown).
*[[1781]] &amp;ndash; [[American Revolutionary War]]: A small force of Pennsylvania [[militia (United States)|militia]] is [[Lochry's Defeat|ambushed and overwhelmed]] by an American Indian group, which forces [[George Rogers Clark]] to abandon his attempt to attack [[Detroit]].
*[[1812]] &amp;ndash; [[Peninsular War]]: A coalition of Spanish, [[United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland|British]], and [[Kingdom of Portugal|Portuguese]] forces succeed in lifting the two-and-a-half-year-long [[Siege of Cádiz]].
*[[1814]] &amp;ndash; British troops invade Washington, D.C. and during the [[Burning of Washington]] the [[White House]], the [[United States Capitol|Capitol]] and many other buildings are set ablaze.
*[[1815]] &amp;ndash; The modern [[Constitution of the Netherlands]] is signed.
*[[1816]] &amp;ndash; The [[Treaty of St. Louis (1816)|Treaty of St. Louis]] is signed in [[St. Louis, Missouri]].
*[[1820]] &amp;ndash; [[Constitutionalism|Constitutionalist]] [[History of Portugal#Crises of the nineteenth century|insurrection]] at [[Porto|Oporto]], Portugal.
*[[1821]] &amp;ndash; The [[Treaty of Córdoba]] is signed in [[Córdoba, Veracruz|Córdoba]], now in [[Veracruz (state)|Veracruz]], Mexico, concluding the [[Mexican War of Independence]] from Spain.
*[[1857]] &amp;ndash; The [[Panic of 1857]] begins, setting off one of the most severe economic crises in United States history.
*[[1870]] &amp;ndash; The [[Wolseley expedition]] reaches [[Manitoba]] to end the [[Red River Rebellion]].
*[[1875]] &amp;ndash; Captain [[Matthew Webb]] became the first person to swim the [[English Channel]].
*[[1891]] &amp;ndash; [[Thomas Edison]] patents the motion picture camera.
*[[1898]] &amp;ndash; [[Mikhail Nikolayevich Muravyov|Count Muravyov]], [[Foreign Minister of Russia]] presents a ''rescript'' that convoked the [[First Hague Peace Conference]].
*[[1909]] &amp;ndash; Workers start pouring concrete for the [[Panama Canal]].
*[[1911]] &amp;ndash; [[Manuel de Arriaga]] is elected and sworn-in as the first [[President of Portugal]].
*[[1914]] &amp;ndash; [[World War I]]: German troops capture [[Namur, Belgium|Namur]].
*  1914   &amp;ndash; World War I: The [[Battle of Cer]] ends as the first [[Allies of World War I|Allied]] victory in the war.
*[[1929]] &amp;ndash; Second day of two-day [[1929 Hebron massacre|Hebron massacre]] during the [[1929 Palestine riots]]: [[Palestinians|Arab]] attacks on the Jewish community in [[Hebron]] in the [[British Mandate of Palestine]], result in the death of 65–68 Jews; the remaining Jews are forced to flee the city.
*[[1931]] &amp;ndash; France and the [[Soviet Union]] sign a neutrality pact.
*  1931   &amp;ndash; Resignation of the United Kingdom's [[Second Labour Government]]. Formation of the [[UK National Government]].
*[[1932]] &amp;ndash; [[Amelia Earhart]] becomes the first woman to fly across the United States non-stop (from Los Angeles to [[Newark, New Jersey]]).
*[[1933]] &amp;ndash; The [[Crescent (train)|Crescent Limited]] train [[Amtrak Railroad Anacostia Bridge#Wreck of the Crescent Limited|derails]] in Washington, D.C., after the [[Amtrak Railroad Anacostia Bridge|bridge]] it is crossing is washed out by the [[1933 Chesapeake–Potomac hurricane]].
*[[1936]] &amp;ndash; The [[Australian Antarctic Territory]] is created.
*[[1937]] &amp;ndash; [[Spanish Civil War]]: the [[Basque Army]] surrenders to the Italian [[Corpo Truppe Volontarie]] following the [[Santoña Agreement]].
*  1937 &amp;ndash; Spanish Civil War: [[Sovereign Council of Asturias and León]] is proclaimed in [[Gijón]].
*[[1941]] &amp;ndash; [[Adolf Hitler]] orders the cessation of [[Nazi Germany]]'s systematic [[Action T4|T4 euthanasia program]] of the [[mental illness|mentally ill]] and the [[Disability|handicapped]] due to protests, although killings continue for the remainder of the war.
*[[1942]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: The [[Battle of the Eastern Solomons]]. Japanese [[aircraft carrier]] ''[[Japanese aircraft carrier Ryūjō|Ryūjō]]'' is sunk, with the loss of 7 officers and 113 crewmen. The US carrier {{USS|Enterprise|CV-6|6}} is heavily damaged.
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; World War II: Allied troops begin [[Liberation of Paris|the attack on Paris]].
*[[1949]] &amp;ndash; The treaty creating the [[NATO|North Atlantic Treaty Organization]] goes into effect.
*[[1950]] &amp;ndash; [[Edith Sampson]] becomes the first black U.S. delegate to the [[United Nations]].
*[[1954]] &amp;ndash; The [[Communist Control Act]] goes into effect, outlawing the [[American Communist Party]].
*  1954   &amp;ndash; [[Getúlio Dornelles Vargas]], president of [[Brazil]], commits suicide and is succeeded by [[João Café Filho]].
*[[1963]] &amp;ndash; [[Buddhist crisis]]: As a result of the [[Xá Lợi Pagoda raids]], the [[US State Department]] [[Cable 243|cables]] the [[United States Embassy, Saigon]] to encourage [[Army of the Republic of Vietnam]] generals to [[1963 South Vietnamese coup|launch a coup]] against President [[Ngô Đình Diệm]] if he did not remove his brother [[Ngô Đình Nhu]].
*[[1967]] &amp;ndash; Led by [[Abbie Hoffman]], the [[Youth International Party]] temporarily disrupts trading at the [[New York Stock Exchange]] by throwing dollar bills from the viewing gallery, causing trading to cease as brokers scramble to grab them.
*[[1970]] &amp;ndash; [[Vietnam War]] protesters [[Sterling Hall bombing|bomb]] Sterling Hall at the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]], leading to an international manhunt for the perpetrators.
*[[1981]] &amp;ndash; [[Mark David Chapman]] is sentenced to 20 years to life in prison for murdering [[John Lennon]].
*[[1989]] &amp;ndash; [[Colombia]]n [[drug baron]]s declare &quot;total war&quot; on the [[Government of Colombia|Colombian government]].
*  1989   &amp;ndash; [[Cincinnati Reds]] [[Manager (baseball)|manager]] [[Pete Rose]] is banned from [[baseball]] for gambling by [[Commissioner of Baseball (MLB)|Commissioner]] [[A. Bartlett Giamatti]].
*  1989   &amp;ndash; [[Tadeusz Mazowiecki]] is chosen as the first non-[[communist]] [[prime minister]] in Central and Eastern Europe.
*[[1991]] &amp;ndash; [[Mikhail Gorbachev]] resigns as head of the [[Communist Party of the Soviet Union]].
*  1991   &amp;ndash; [[Ukraine]] [[Declaration of Independence of Ukraine|declares itself independent]] from the [[Soviet Union]].
*[[1992]] &amp;ndash; [[Hurricane Andrew]] makes landfall in [[Homestead, Florida]] as a [[List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes|Category&amp;nbsp;5 hurricane]], causing up to $25&amp;nbsp;billion (1992 [[USD]]) in damages.
*[[1994]] &amp;ndash; Initial accord between [[Israel]] and the [[PLO]] about partial self-rule of the [[Palestinians]] on the [[West Bank]].
*[[1995]] &amp;ndash; Microsoft [[Windows 95]] was released to the public in North America.
*[[1998]] &amp;ndash; First [[radio-frequency identification]] (RFID) human implantation tested in the United Kingdom.
*[[2004]] &amp;ndash; Eighty-nine passengers die after [[Russian aircraft bombings of August 2004|two airliners explode]] after flying out of [[Domodedovo International Airport]], near Moscow. The explosions are caused by suicide bombers from [[Chechnya]].
*[[2006]] &amp;ndash; The [[International Astronomical Union]] (IAU) [[IAU definition of planet|redefines the term &quot;planet&quot;]] such that [[Pluto]] is now considered a [[dwarf planet]].
*[[2010]] &amp;ndash; In [[San Fernando, Tamaulipas]], Mexico, 72 illegal immigrants are [[2010 San Fernando massacre|killed]] by [[Los Zetas]] and eventually found dead by Mexican authorities.
*[[2016]] &amp;ndash; An [[August 2016 Central Italy earthquake|earthquake strikes Central Italy]] with a magnitude of 6.2, with aftershocks felt as far as [[Rome]] and [[Florence]].
