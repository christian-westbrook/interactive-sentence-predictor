==Etymology==
The name ''Alexander'' is derived from the [[Greek language|Greek]] {{lang|grc-gre|·ºàŒªŒ≠ŒæŒ±ŒΩŒ¥œÅŒøœÇ}} (Al√©xandros), meaning &quot;Defender of the people&quot; or &quot;Defending men&quot;&lt;ref&gt;{{LSJ|a)le/candros|·ºàŒªŒ≠ŒæŒ±ŒΩŒ¥œÅŒøœÇ|ref}}.&lt;/ref&gt; and also, &quot;Protector of men&quot;, a compound of the verb {{lang|grc|·ºÄŒªŒ≠ŒæŒµŒπŒΩ}} ''alexein'', &quot;to ward off, to avert, to defend&quot;&lt;ref&gt;{{LSJ|a)le/cw|·ºÄŒªŒ≠ŒæŒµŒπŒΩ|shortref}}.&lt;/ref&gt; and the noun {{lang|grc|·ºÄŒΩŒÆœÅ}} ''anƒìr'', &quot;man&quot; (&lt;small&gt;[[Genitive|GEN]]&lt;/small&gt; {{lang|grc|·ºÄŒΩŒ¥œÅœåœÇ}} ''andros'').&lt;ref&gt;{{LSJ|a)nh/r|·ºÄŒΩŒÆœÅ|shortref}}.&lt;/ref&gt; It is an example of the widespread motif of Greek (or Indo-European more generally) names expressing &quot;battle-prowess&quot;, in this case the ability to withstand or push back an enemy [[shield wall|battle line]].{{citation needed|date=November 2012}}

The name was one of the titles (&quot;epithets&quot;) given to the Greek goddess [[Hera]] and as such is usually taken to mean &quot;one who comes to save warriors&quot;. In the [[Iliad]], the character [[Paris (mythology)|Paris]] is known also as Alexander.&lt;ref&gt;[http://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus%3Atext%3A1999.04.0073%3Aentry%3D*%29ale%2Fcandros ·ºàŒªŒ≠ŒæŒ±ŒΩŒ¥œÅŒøœÇ],

Most later Alexanders in various countries were directly or indirectly named for him.{{citation needed|date=June 2010}}
