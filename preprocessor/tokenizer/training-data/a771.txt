&quot;'''The Triumph of Time'''&quot; is a poem by [[Algernon Charles Swinburne]], published in ''[[Poems and Ballads]]'' in 1866. It is in adapted [[ottava rima]] and is full of elaborate use of literary devices, particularly [[alliteration]]. The theme, which purports to be autobiographical, is that of rejected love. The speaker deplores the ruin of his life, and in tones at times reminiscent of ''[[Hamlet]]'', craves oblivion, for which the sea serves as a constant metaphor.
