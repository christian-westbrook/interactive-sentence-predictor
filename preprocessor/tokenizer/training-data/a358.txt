==Events==
* [[311]] &amp;ndash; The [[Diocletianic Persecution]] of Christians in the [[Roman Empire]] ends.
* [[313]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Tzirallum]]: Emperor [[Licinius]] defeats [[Maximinus II]] and unifies the [[Eastern Roman Empire]].
* [[642]] &amp;ndash; [[Chindasuinth]] is proclaimed king by the [[Visigoths|Visigothic]] nobility and bishops.
*[[1315]] &amp;ndash; [[Enguerrand de Marigny]] is hanged at the instigation of [[Charles, Count of Valois]].
*[[1492]] &amp;ndash; Spain gives [[Christopher Columbus]] his commission of exploration.
*[[1513]] &amp;ndash; [[Edmund de la Pole, 3rd Duke of Suffolk|Edmund de la Pole]], Yorkist pretender to the English throne, is executed on the orders of [[Henry VIII of England|Henry VIII]].
*[[1557]] &amp;ndash; [[Mapuche]] leader [[Lautaro]] is killed by [[Spanish Empire|Spanish]] forces at the [[Battle of Mataquito]] in [[Chile]].
*[[1598]] &amp;ndash; [[Juan de Oñate]] makes a formal declaration of his Conquest of New Mexico.
*[[1598]] &amp;ndash; [[Henry IV of France]] issues the [[Edict of Nantes]], allowing [[freedom of religion]] to the [[Huguenot]]s.
*[[1636]] &amp;ndash; [[Eighty Years' War]]: [[Dutch Republic]] forces recapture a strategically important fort from Spain after [[Siege of Schenkenschans|a nine-month siege]].
*[[1671]] &amp;ndash; [[Petar Zrinski]], the [[Croatia]]n [[Ban (title)|Ban]] from the [[Zrinski]] family, is [[capital punishment|executed]].
*[[1789]] &amp;ndash; On the balcony of [[Federal Hall]] on [[Wall Street]] in New York City, [[George Washington]] takes the [[First inauguration of George Washington|oath of office]] to become the first elected [[President of the United States]].
*[[1803]] &amp;ndash; [[Louisiana Purchase]]: The United States purchases the [[Louisiana Territory]] from France for $15&amp;nbsp;million, more than doubling the size of the young nation.
*[[1812]] &amp;ndash; The [[Territory of Orleans]] becomes the 18th [[U.S. state]] under the name [[Louisiana]].
*[[1838]] &amp;ndash; [[Nicaragua]] declares independence from the [[Central American Federation]].
*[[1863]] &amp;ndash; A 65-man [[French Foreign Legion]] infantry patrol fights a force of nearly 2,000 Mexican soldiers to nearly the last man in [[Battle of Camarón|Hacienda Camarón]], Mexico.
*[[1871]] &amp;ndash; The [[Camp Grant massacre]] takes place in [[Arizona Territory]].
*[[1885]] &amp;ndash; [[Governor of New York]] [[David B. Hill]] signs legislation creating the [[Niagara Reservation State Park|Niagara Reservation]], New York's first state park, ensuring that [[Niagara Falls]] will not be devoted solely to industrial and commercial use.
*[[1897]] &amp;ndash; [[J. J. Thomson]] of the [[Cavendish Laboratory]] announces his discovery of the [[electron]] as a [[subatomic particle]], over 1,800 times smaller than a [[proton]] (in the atomic nucleus), at a lecture at the [[Royal Institution]] in London.&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite journal|title=Ninety years around the atom|first=Christine|last=Sutton|journal=New Scientist|date=8 January 1997|page=49}}&lt;/ref&gt;
*[[1900]] &amp;ndash; Hawaii becomes a territory of the United States, with [[Sanford B. Dole]] as [[Governor of Hawaii|governor]].
*[[1904]] &amp;ndash; The [[Louisiana Purchase Exposition]] [[World's Fair]] opens in [[St. Louis, Missouri]].
* [[1905]] &amp;ndash; [[Albert Einstein]] writes his thesis [http://e-collection.library.ethz.ch/eserv/eth:30378/eth-30378-01.pdf ''Eine neue Bestimmung der Moleküldimensionen'']  (&quot;A New Determination of Molecular Dimensions&quot;).
*[[1907]] &amp;ndash; [[Honolulu, Hawaii]] becomes an independent city.&lt;!--Independent of whom?--&gt;
*[[1925]] &amp;ndash; Automaker [[Dodge Brothers, Inc]] is sold to [[Dillon, Read &amp; Co.]] for US$146&amp;nbsp;million plus $50&amp;nbsp;million for charity.
*[[1927]] &amp;ndash; The [[Federal Industrial Institute for Women]] opens in [[Alderson, West Virginia]], as the first women's federal prison in the United States.
*  1927   &amp;ndash; [[Douglas Fairbanks]] and [[Mary Pickford]] become the first celebrities to leave their footprints in concrete at [[Grauman's Chinese Theater]] in [[Hollywood]].
*[[1937]] &amp;ndash; The [[Commonwealth of the Philippines]] holds a [[Philippine women's suffrage plebiscite, 1937|plebiscite for Filipino women]] on whether they should be extended the right to suffrage; over 90% would vote in the affirmative.
*[[1938]] &amp;ndash; The [[animated cartoon]] short ''[[Porky's Hare Hunt]]'' debuts in movie theaters, introducing [[Happy Rabbit]] (a prototype of [[Evolution of Bugs Bunny|Bugs Bunny]]).
*[[1939]] &amp;ndash; The [[1939 New York World's Fair|1939-40 New York World's Fair]] opens.
*  1939   &amp;ndash; [[NBC]] inaugurates its regularly scheduled [[History of television#Broadcast television|television]] service in New York City, broadcasting President [[Franklin D. Roosevelt]]'s [[1939 New York World's Fair|N.Y. World's Fair]] opening day ceremonial address.
*[[1943]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: The British submarine {{HMS|Seraph|P219|6}} surfaces near [[Huelva]] to cast adrift a dead man dressed as a courier and carrying [[Operation Mincemeat|false invasion plans]].
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; World War II: ''[[Führerbunker]]'': [[Adolf Hitler]] and [[Eva Braun]] commit suicide after being married for less than 40 hours. [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] soldiers raise the [[Victory Banner]] over the [[Reichstag building]].
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Stalag Luft I]] prisoner-of-war camp near Barth, Germany is liberated by Soviet soldiers, freeing nearly 9000 American and British airmen.
*[[1947]] &amp;ndash; In [[Nevada]], Boulder Dam is renamed [[Hoover Dam]] for the second time.
*[[1948]] &amp;ndash; In [[Bogotá, Colombia]], the [[Organization of American States]] is established.
*[[1956]] &amp;ndash; Former Vice President and Democratic Senator [[Alben Barkley]] dies during a speech in [[Virginia]].
*[[1957]] &amp;ndash; [[Supplementary Convention on the Abolition of Slavery]] entered into force.
*[[1961]] &amp;ndash; ''[[Soviet submarine K-19|K-19]]'', the first [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] [[nuclear submarine]] equipped with [[Submarine-launched ballistic missile|nuclear missiles]], is [[ship commissioning|commissioned]].
*[[1963]] &amp;ndash; The [[Bristol Bus Boycott]] is held in [[Bristol]] to protest the [[Bristol Omnibus Company]]'s refusal to employ Black or Asian bus crews, drawing national attention to racial discrimination in the United Kingdom.
*[[1973]] &amp;ndash; [[Watergate scandal]]: U.S. President [[Richard Nixon]] announces that [[White House]] Counsel [[John Dean]] has been fired and that other top aides, most notably [[H. R. Haldeman]] and [[John Ehrlichman]], have resigned.
*[[1975]] &amp;ndash; [[Fall of Saigon]]: [[Communist]] forces gain control of [[Saigon]]. The [[Vietnam War]] formally ends with the unconditional surrender of [[South Vietnam]]ese president [[Dương Văn Minh]].
*[[1980]] &amp;ndash; The [[Iranian Embassy siege]] begins in London.
*[[1982]] &amp;ndash; The [[Bijon Setu massacre]] occurs in [[Calcutta]].
*[[1992]] &amp;ndash; [[Nickelodeon Studios]] in Orlando, Florida bury a time capsule to be opened in 2042; 50 years after its burial.
*[[1993]] &amp;ndash; [[CERN]] announces [[World Wide Web]] protocols will be free.
*[[1994]] &amp;ndash; [[Formula One]] racing driver [[Roland Ratzenberger]] is killed in a crash during the qualifying session of the [[San Marino Grand Prix]] run at [[Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari]] outside [[Imola]], Italy.
*[[1997]] &amp;ndash; [[Ellen DeGeneres]] [[Coming out|came out]] as gay. Her sitcom, ''[[Ellen (TV series)|Ellen]]'', became one of first major television shows featuring an openly gay main character.
*[[2000]] &amp;ndash; [[Canonization]] of [[Faustina Kowalska]] in the presence of 200,000 people and the first [[Divine Mercy Sunday]] celebrated worldwide.
*[[2004]] &amp;ndash; U.S. media release graphic photos of American soldiers abusing and sexually humiliating [[Iraq]]i prisoners at [[Abu Ghraib]] prison.
*[[2008]] &amp;ndash; Two skeletal remains found near [[Yekaterinburg]], Russia are confirmed by Russian scientists to be the remains of [[Alexei Nikolaevich, Tsarevich of Russia|Alexei]] and [[Grand Duchess Anastasia Nikolaevna of Russia|Anastasia]], two of the children of the last Tsar of Russia, whose entire family was executed at [[Yekaterinburg]] by the Bolsheviks.
*[[2009]] &amp;ndash; [[Chrysler]] files for [[Chapter 11, Title 11, United States Code|Chapter 11]] bankruptcy.
*  2009   &amp;ndash; Seven civilians and the perpetrator are [[2009 attack on the Dutch Royal Family|killed]] and another ten injured at a [[Koninginnedag|Queen's Day]] parade in [[Apeldoorn]], Netherlands in an attempted assassination on Queen [[Beatrix of the Netherlands|Beatrix]].
*[[2012]] &amp;ndash; An overloaded [[Assam ferry sinking|ferry capsizes]] on the [[Brahmaputra River]] in India killing at least 103 people.
*[[2014]] &amp;ndash; A [[April 2014 Ürümqi attack|bomb blast]] in [[Ürümqi]] kills three people and injures 79 others.
