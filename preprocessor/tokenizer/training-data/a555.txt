This day marks the approximate midpoint of summer in the [[Northern Hemisphere]] and of winter in the [[Southern Hemisphere]] (starting the season at the June solstice).

==Events==
*[[322 BC]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Crannon]] between [[Athens]] and [[Macedonia (ancient kingdom)|Macedonia]].
* [[461]] &amp;ndash; Roman Emperor [[Majorian]] is beheaded near the river [[Staffora|Iria]] in north-west Italy following his arrest and deposition by the ''magister militum'' [[Ricimer]].
* [[626]] &amp;ndash; The [[Avars (Carpathians)|Avar]] and [[Slavs|Slav]] armies leave the [[siege of Constantinople (626)|siege of Constantinople]].
* 768 - Stephen III begins his reign as Catholic Pope.
* [[936]] &amp;ndash; Coronation of King [[Otto I, Holy Roman Emperor|Otto I of Germany]].
*[[1420]] &amp;ndash; Construction of the dome of [[Florence Cathedral|Santa Maria del Fiore]] begins in [[Florence]].
*[[1427]] &amp;ndash; The [[House of Visconti|Visconti]] of Milan's fleet is destroyed by the [[Republic of Venice|Venetians]] on the [[Po (river)|Po River]].
*[[1461]] &amp;ndash; The [[Ming dynasty]] [[Military history of China before 1911|Chinese military general]] Cao Qin [[Rebellion of Cao Qin|stages a coup]] against the [[Zhengtong Emperor|Tianshun Emperor]].
* 1479 - [[Battle of Guinegate]],French troops of King [[Louis XI]] is defeated by the Burgundians led by Archduke Maximilian of Habsburg.
*[[1679]] &amp;ndash; The [[brigantine]] ''[[Le Griffon]]'', commissioned by [[René-Robert Cavelier, Sieur de La Salle]], is towed to the south-eastern end of the [[Niagara River]], to become the first ship to sail the upper [[Great Lakes]] of North America.
*[[1714]] &amp;ndash; The [[Battle of Gangut]]: The first important victory of the [[Russian Navy]].
*[[1782]] &amp;ndash; [[George Washington]] orders the creation of the [[Badge of Military Merit]] to honor soldiers wounded in battle. It is later renamed to the more poetic [[Purple Heart]].
*[[1789]] &amp;ndash; The [[United States Department of War]] is established.
*[[1791]] &amp;ndash; American troops destroy the [[Miami people|Miami]] town of [[Battle of Kenapacomaqua|Kenapacomaqua]] near the site of present-day [[Logansport, Indiana]] in the [[Northwest Indian War]].
*[[1794]] &amp;ndash; [[President of the United States|U.S. President]] George Washington invokes the [[Militia Acts of 1792]] to suppress the [[Whiskey Rebellion]] in [[western Pennsylvania]].
*[[1819]] &amp;ndash; [[Simón Bolívar]] triumphs over Spain in the [[Battle of Boyacá]].
*[[1858]] &amp;ndash; The first [[Australian rules football]] match is played between [[Melbourne Grammar]] and Scotch College.
*[[1879]] &amp;ndash; The opening of the [[Openshaw Citadel|Poor Man's Palace]] in [[Manchester|Manchester, England]].
*[[1890]] &amp;ndash; [[Anna Månsdotter]] becomes the last woman in Sweden to be executed, for the 1889 [[Yngsjö murder]].
*[[1909]] &amp;ndash; [[Alice Huyler Ramsey]] and three friends become the first women to complete a transcontinental auto trip, taking 59 days to travel from [[New York City|New York, New York]] to [[San Francisco|San Francisco, California]].
*[[1927]] &amp;ndash; The [[Peace Bridge]] opens between [[Fort Erie, Ontario]] and [[Buffalo, New York]].
*[[1930]] &amp;ndash; The last confirmed [[lynching]] of blacks in the Northern United States occurs in [[Marion, Indiana]]; two men, [[Thomas Shipp and Abram Smith]], are killed.
*[[1933]] &amp;ndash; The [[Simele massacre]]: The [[Politics of Iraq|Iraqi government]] [[Simele massacre|slaughters]] over 3,000 [[Assyrian people|Assyrians]] in the village of [[Simele]].
*[[1938]] &amp;ndash; The building of [[Mauthausen-Gusen concentration camp|Mauthausen concentration camp]] begins.
*[[1940]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: [[Alsace-Lorraine]] is annexed by the Third Reich.
*[[1942]] &amp;ndash; World War II: The [[Guadalcanal Campaign|Battle of Guadalcanal]] begins as the [[United States Marine Corps|United States Marines]] initiate the first American offensive of the war with landings on [[Guadalcanal]] and [[Battle of Tulagi and Gavutu–Tanambogo|Tulagi]] in the [[Solomon Islands]].
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; [[IBM]] dedicates the first program-controlled [[calculator]], the Automatic Sequence Controlled Calculator (known best as the [[Harvard Mark I]]).
*[[1946]] &amp;ndash; The government of the [[Soviet Union]] presented a note to its [[Turkey|Turkish counterparts]] which refuted the latter's sovereignty over the [[Turkish Straits]], thus beginning the [[Turkish Straits crisis]].
*[[1947]] &amp;ndash; [[Thor Heyerdahl]]'s [[Ochroma pyramidale|balsa wood]] raft the [[Kon-Tiki]], smashes into the [[reef]] at [[Raroia]] in the [[Tuamotus|Tuamotu Islands]] after a 101-day, {{Convert|7000|km|mi}} journey across the Pacific Ocean in an attempt to prove that pre-historic peoples could have traveled from South America.
*  1947   &amp;ndash; The [[Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai|Bombay Municipal Corporation]] formally takes over the [[Brihanmumbai Electric Supply and Transport|Bombay Electric Supply and Transport]] (BEST).
*[[1955]] &amp;ndash; [[Sony|Tokyo Telecommunications Engineering]], the precursor to [[Sony]], sells its first [[transistor radio]]s in Japan.
*[[1959]] &amp;ndash; The [[Lincoln Memorial]] design on the U.S. [[Penny (United States coin)|penny]] goes into circulation. It replaces the &quot;sheaves of wheat&quot; design, and was minted until 2008.
*  1959   &amp;ndash; [[Explorer program]]: ''[[Explorer 6]]'' launches from the [[Eastern Range|Atlantic Missile Range]] in [[Cape Canaveral, Florida]].
*[[1960]] &amp;ndash; [[Ivory Coast]] becomes independent from France.
*[[1962]] &amp;ndash; Canadian-born American pharmacologist [[Frances Oldham Kelsey]] awarded the U.S. President's Award for Distinguished Federal Civilian Service for her refusal to authorize [[thalidomide]].
*[[1964]] &amp;ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: The [[United States Congress|U.S. Congress]] passes the [[Gulf of Tonkin Resolution]] giving U.S. President [[Lyndon B. Johnson]] broad war powers to deal with [[North Vietnam]]ese attacks on American forces.
*[[1970]] &amp;ndash; California judge [[Harold Haley]] is taken hostage in his courtroom and killed during an effort to free [[George Jackson (Black Panther)|George Jackson]] from police custody.
*[[1974]] &amp;ndash; [[Philippe Petit]] performs a high wire act between the twin towers of the [[World Trade Center (1973-2001)|World Trade Center]] {{Convert|1368|ft|m}} in the air.
*[[1976]] &amp;ndash; [[Viking program]]: ''[[Viking 2]]'' enters orbit around [[Mars]].
*[[1978]] &amp;ndash; U.S. President [[Jimmy Carter]] declares a federal emergency at [[Love Canal]] due to [[toxic waste]] that had been disposed of negligently.
*[[1981]] &amp;ndash; ''[[The Washington Star]]'' ceases all operations after 128 years of publication.
*[[1985]] &amp;ndash; [[Takao Doi]], [[Mamoru Mohri]] and [[Chiaki Mukai]] are chosen to be Japan's first [[astronaut]]s.
*[[1987]] &amp;ndash; [[Lynne Cox]] becomes first person to swim from the United States to the [[Soviet Union]], crossing the [[Bering Strait]] from [[Little Diomede Island]] in Alaska to [[Big Diomede]] in the Soviet Union
*[[1989]] &amp;ndash; U.S. Congressman [[Mickey Leland]] (D-[[Texas|TX]]) and 15 others die in a [[Aviation accidents and incidents|plane crash]] in [[Ethiopia]].
*[[1990]] &amp;ndash; First American soldiers arrive in Saudi Arabia as part of the [[Gulf War]].
*[[1998]] &amp;ndash; [[1998 United States embassy bombings|Bombings at  United States embassies]] in [[Dar es Salaam|Dar es Salaam, Tanzania]] and [[Nairobi|Nairobi, Kenya]] kill approximately 212 people.
*[[1999]] &amp;ndash; The [[Chechnya]]-based [[Islamic International Brigade]] [[War of Dagestan|invades]] neighboring [[Dagestan]].
*[[2008]] &amp;ndash; The start of the [[Russo-Georgian War]] over the territory of [[South Ossetia]].
