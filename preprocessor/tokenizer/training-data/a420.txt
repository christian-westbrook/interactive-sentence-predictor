==Events==
*[[29 BC]] &amp;ndash; [[Octavian]] holds the first of three consecutive [[Roman triumph|triumphs]] in [[Ancient Rome|Rome]] to celebrate the victory over the [[Dalmatian tribes]].
* [[523]] &amp;ndash; [[Pope John I|John I]] becomes the new [[Pope]] after the death of [[Pope Hormisdas]].
* [[554]] &amp;ndash; Emperor [[Justinian I]] rewards [[Liberius (praetorian prefect)|Liberius]] for his long and distinguished service in the [[Pragmatic Sanction]], granting him extensive [[Estate (land)|estate]]s in Italy.
* [[582]] &amp;ndash; [[Maurice (emperor)|Maurice]] becomes [[Roman emperor|Emperor]] of the [[Eastern Roman Empire]].
* [[900]] &amp;ndash; Count [[Reginar, Duke of Lorraine|Reginar I of Hainault]] rises against [[Zwentibold]] of [[Lotharingia]] and slays him near present-day [[Susteren]].
*[[1099]] &amp;ndash; Raniero is elected as [[Pope Paschal II]].
*[[1516]] &amp;ndash; The [[War of the League of Cambrai|Treaty of Noyon]] between France and Spain is signed. [[Francis I of France]] recognizes Charles's claim to [[Naples]], and [[Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor]], recognizes Francis's claim to [[Milan]].
*[[1521]] &amp;ndash; After [[Fall of Tenochtitlan|an extended siege]], forces led by Spanish [[conquistador]] [[Hernán Cortés]] capture [[Tlatoani]] [[Cuauhtémoc]] and conquer the [[Aztec]] capital of [[Tenochtitlan]].
*[[1532]] &amp;ndash; [[Union of Brittany and France]]: The [[Duchy of Brittany]] is absorbed into the [[Kingdom of France]].
*[[1536]] &amp;ndash; [[Buddhism|Buddhist]] monks from [[Kyoto]], Japan's [[Enryaku-ji]] temple set fire to 21 [[Nichiren Buddhism|Nichiren]] temples throughout in what will be known as the Tenbun Hokke Disturbance. (Traditional [[Japanese calendar|Japanese date]]: July 27, 1536).
*[[1553]] &amp;ndash; [[Michael Servetus]] is arrested by [[John Calvin]] in [[Geneva]], Switzerland as a [[Heresy in Christianity|heretic]].
*[[1624]] &amp;ndash; The French king [[Louis XIII]] appoints [[Cardinal Richelieu]] as prime minister.
*[[1645]] &amp;ndash; Sweden and Denmark sign [[Peace of Brömsebro]].
*[[1704]] &amp;ndash; [[War of the Spanish Succession]]: [[Battle of Blenheim]]: [[Kingdom of England|English]] and [[Holy Roman Empire|Imperial]] forces are victorious over [[Kingdom of France|French]] and [[Electorate of Bavaria|Bavaria]]n troops.
*[[1779]] &amp;ndash; [[American Revolutionary War]]: The [[Royal Navy]] defeats the [[Penobscot Expedition]] with the most significant loss of United States naval forces prior to the [[attack on Pearl Harbor]].
*[[1792]] &amp;ndash; King [[Louis XVI of France]] is formally arrested by the National Tribunal, and declared an [[enemy of the people]].
*[[1806]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Mišar]] during the [[Serbian Revolution]] begins. The battle will end two days later, with a decisive Serbian victory over the Ottomans.
*[[1814]] &amp;ndash; The [[Anglo-Dutch Treaty of 1814|Convention of London]], a treaty between the United Kingdom and the [[Dutch Republic|United Provinces]], is signed in London, England.
*[[1868]] &amp;ndash; The 8.5–9.0 {{M|w}} [[1868 Arica earthquake|Arica earthquake]] struck southern [[Peru]] with a maximum [[Mercalli intensity scale|Mercalli intensity]] of XI (''Extreme''), causing 25,000+ deaths and a destructive basin wide [[tsunami]] that affected Hawaii and New Zealand.
*[[1889]] &amp;ndash; William Gray of [[Hartford]], Connecticut is granted United States Patent Number 408,709 for &quot;Coin-controlled apparatus for telephones.&quot; &lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://docs.google.com/viewer?url=patentimages.storage.googleapis.com/pdfs/US408709.pdf |title=Coin-controlled apparatus for telephones |website=google.com |access-date=13 August 2017}}&lt;/ref&gt;
*[[1898]] &amp;ndash; [[Spanish–American War]]: Spanish and American forces engage in a [[Battle of Manila (1898)|mock battle]] for [[Manila]], after which the Spanish commander surrendered in order to keep the city out of Filipino rebel hands.
*  1898   &amp;ndash; [[Carl Gustav Witt]] discovers [[433 Eros]], the first [[Near-Earth object#Near-Earth asteroids|near-Earth asteroid]] to be found.
* [[1905]] &amp;ndash; Norwegians [[Norwegian union dissolution referendum, 1905|vote]] to end the union with Sweden.
*[[1906]] &amp;ndash; The all black infantrymen of the U.S. Army's [[25th Infantry Regiment (United States)|25th Infantry Regiment]] [[Brownsville Affair|are accused]] of killing a white bartender and wounding a white police officer in [[Brownsville, Texas]], despite exculpatory evidence; all are later dishonorably discharged. (Their records were later restored to reflect honorable discharges but there were no financial settlements.
*  1913   &amp;ndash; First production in the UK of [[stainless steel]] by [[Harry Brearley]].
*[[1918]] &amp;ndash; Women enlist in the [[United States Marine Corps]] for the first time. [[Opha May Johnson]] is the first woman to enlist.
*  1918   &amp;ndash; Bayerische Motoren Werke AG ([[BMW]]) [[History of BMW#BMW GmbH goes public|established as a public company]] in Germany.
*[[1920]] &amp;ndash; [[Polish–Soviet War]]: The [[Battle of Warsaw (1920)|Battle of Warsaw]] begins and will last till [[August 25]]. The [[Red Army]] is defeated.
*[[1937]] &amp;ndash; The [[Battle of Shanghai]] begins.
*[[1942]] &amp;ndash; Major General [[Eugene Reybold]] of the [[United States Army Corps of Engineers|U.S. Army Corps of Engineers]] authorizes the construction of facilities that would house the &quot;Development of Substitute Materials&quot; project, better known as the [[Manhattan Project]].
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: German troops begin the [[Razing of Anogeia|pillage and razing of Anogeia]] in [[Crete]] that would continue until [[September 5]].
*[[1954]] &amp;ndash; [[Pakistan Broadcasting Corporation|Radio Pakistan]] broadcasts the &quot;[[Qaumī Tarāna]]&quot;, the [[national anthem]] of [[Pakistan]] for the first time.
*[[1960]] &amp;ndash; The [[Central African Republic]] declares independence from France.
*[[1961]] &amp;ndash; [[East Germany]] closes the border between the eastern and western sectors of Berlin to thwart its inhabitants' attempts to escape to the [[Western world|West]], and construction of the [[Berlin Wall]] is started.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://www.berlin.de/mauer/en/history/construction-of-the-berlin-wall/ |title=Construction of the Berlin Wall |website=Berlin.de |access-date=12 August 2017}}&lt;/ref&gt;
*[[1964]] &amp;ndash; Peter Allen and Gwynne Evans are hanged for the [[Murder of John Alan West]] becoming the last people [[Capital punishment in the United Kingdom|executed in the United Kingdom]].
*[[1968]] &amp;ndash; [[Alexandros Panagoulis]] attempts to assassinate the Greek [[Greek military junta of 1967–1974|dictator]] Colonel [[Georgios Papadopoulos]] in [[Varkiza]], [[Athens]].
*[[1969]] &amp;ndash; The ''[[Apollo 11]]'' astronauts are released from a three-week [[quarantine]] to enjoy a [[ticker tape parade]] in New York City That evening, at a [[state dinner]] in [[Los Angeles]], they are awarded the [[Presidential Medal of Freedom]] by U.S. President [[Richard Nixon]].
*[[1977]] &amp;ndash; Members of the [[National Front (UK)|British National Front]] (NF) [[Battle of Lewisham|clash]] with anti-NF demonstrators in [[Lewisham]], London, resulting in 214 arrests and at least 111 injuries.
*[[1978]] &amp;ndash; One hundred fifty [[Palestinian people|Palestinians]] in [[Beirut]] are [[Tel al-Zaatar massacre|killed]] in a [[List of terrorist incidents|terrorist attack]] during the [[Lebanese Civil War#Second phase of the war, 1977-82|second phase]] of the [[Lebanese Civil War]].
*  2004   &amp;ndash; One hundred fifty-six [[Democratic Republic of the Congo|Congolese]] [[Tutsi]] refugees are massacred at the [[Gatumba]] [[refugee camp]] in [[Burundi]].
*[[2008]] &amp;ndash; [[2008 South Ossetia war|South Ossetian War]]: Russian units [[Occupation of Gori|occupy]] the Georgian city of [[Gori, Georgia|Gori]].
*[[2015]] &amp;ndash; At least 76 people are killed and 212 others are wounded in a [[2015 Baghdad market truck bombing|truck bombing]] in [[Baghdad]], Iraq.
