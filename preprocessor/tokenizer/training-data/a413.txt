[[American Samoa]], located within the geographical region of [[Oceania]], is one of only two possessions of the [[United States]] in the [[Southern Hemisphere]], the other being [[Jarvis Island]]. Its total land area is {{convert|76.8|sqmi|km2}}—slightly larger than [[Washington, D.C.]]—consisting of five rugged, volcanic islands and two [[coral atoll]]s. The five volcanic islands are: [[Tutuila]], [[Aunu'u]], [[Ofu-Olosega|Ofu]], [[Ofu-Olosega|Olosega]], [[Tau, American Samoa|Ta'u]]. The coral atolls are: [[Swains Island|Swains]], and [[Rose Atoll]]. Of the seven islands, Rose Atoll is an uninhabited Marine National Monument.

Due to its positioning in the South Pacific Ocean, it is frequently hit by typhoons between December and March.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/resources/the-world-factbook/fields/2021.html#aq |title=Field listing: Natural hazards |author=&lt;!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--&gt; |date= |website=[[CIA World Fact Book]] |publisher=United States Central Intelligence Agency |access-date=2016-10-25 |quote=}}&lt;/ref&gt; Rose Atoll is the easternmost point of the territory. American Samoa is the southernmost part of the United States. American Samoa is home to the [[National Park of American Samoa]].
