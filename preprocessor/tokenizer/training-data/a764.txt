==Events==
*[[30 BC]] &amp;ndash; After the [[Egypt (Roman province)|successful invasion of Egypt]], [[Octavian]] executes [[Marcus Antonius Antyllus]], eldest son of [[Mark Antony]], and [[Caesarion]], the last [[Ptolemaic Kingdom|king of the Ptolemaic dynasty of Egypt]] and only child of [[Julius Caesar]] and [[Cleopatra]].
*[[20 BC]] &amp;ndash; ''[[Ludi]] Volcanalici'' are held within the temple precinct of [[Vulcan (mythology)|Vulcan]], and used by [[Augustus]] to mark the treaty with [[Parthia]] and the return of the [[Aquila (Roman)|legionary standards]] that had been lost at the [[Battle of Carrhae]] in 53 BC.
*[[AD 79]] &amp;ndash; [[Mount Vesuvius]] begins stirring, on the feast day of [[Vulcan (mythology)|Vulcan]], the [[Roman god]] of fire.
* [[406]] &amp;ndash; [[Goths|Gothic]] king [[Radagaisus]] is executed after he is [[Siege of Florence (405)|defeated]] by Roman general [[Stilicho]] and 12,000 &quot;barbarians&quot; are incorporated into the [[Late Roman army|Roman army]] or sold as [[slavery|slaves]].
* [[476]] &amp;ndash; [[Odoacer]], chieftain of the Germanic tribes ([[Heruli]]c - [[Scirii|Scirian]] ''[[foederati]]''), is proclaimed ''rex Italiae'' (&quot;[[King of Italy]]&quot;) by his troops.
* [[634]] &amp;ndash; [[Abu Bakr]] dies at [[Medina]] and is succeeded by [[Umar|Umar I]] who becomes the second [[caliph]] of the [[Rashidun Caliphate]].
*[[1244]] &amp;ndash; [[Siege of Jerusalem (1244)|Siege of Jerusalem]]: The city's citadel, the [[Tower of David]], [[History of Jerusalem (Middle Ages)#Khwarezmian control|surrenders]] to [[Khwarezmian Empire]].
*[[1268]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Tagliacozzo]]: The army of [[Charles of Anjou]] defeats the [[Ghibellines]] supporters of [[Conradin]] of Hohenstaufen marking the fall of the [[Hohenstaufen]] family from the [[Holy Roman Empire|Imperial]] and [[Kingdom of Sicily|Sicilian]] thrones, and leading to the new chapter of [[Capetian House of Anjou|Angevin]] domination in [[Southern Italy]].
*[[1305]] &amp;ndash; [[William Wallace|Sir William Wallace]] is executed for [[high treason]] at [[Smithfield, London]].
*[[1328]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Cassel (1328)|Battle of Cassel]]: French troops stop an uprising of [[Flemish people|Flemish]] farmers.
*[[1382]] &amp;ndash; [[Siege of Moscow (1382)|Siege of Moscow]]: The [[Golden Horde]] led by [[Tokhtamysh]] lays siege to the capital of the [[Grand Duchy of Moscow]].
*[[1514]] &amp;ndash; The [[Battle of Chaldiran]] ends with a decisive victory for the [[Selim I|Sultan Selim I]], [[Ottoman Empire]], over the [[Shah Ismail I]], founder of the [[Safavid dynasty]].
*[[1521]] &amp;ndash; [[Christian II of Denmark]] is deposed as [[king of Sweden]] and [[Gustav I of Sweden|Gustav Vasa]] is elected [[regent]].
*[[1541]] &amp;ndash; French explorer [[Jacques Cartier]] lands near [[Quebec City]] in his third voyage to Canada.
*[[1572]] &amp;ndash; [[French Wars of Religion]]: Mob violence against thousands of [[Huguenot]]s in Paris results in the [[St. Bartholomew's Day massacre]].
*[[1595]] &amp;ndash; [[Long Turkish War]]: [[Wallachian prince]] [[Michael the Brave]] confronts the [[Ottoman Empire|Ottoman]] army in the [[Battle of Călugăreni]] and achieves a [[tactical victory]].
*[[1600]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Gifu Castle]]: The eastern forces of [[Tokugawa Ieyasu]] defeat the western [[Sengoku period|Japanese]] clans loyal to [[Toyotomi Hideyori]], leading to the destruction of [[Gifu Castle]] and serving as a prelude to the [[Battle of Sekigahara]].
*[[1614]] &amp;ndash; [[Fettmilch Uprising]]: Jews are expelled from [[Frankfurt]], [[Holy Roman Empire]], following the plundering of the [[Frankfurter Judengasse|Judengasse]].
*[[1628]] &amp;ndash; [[George Villiers, 1st Duke of Buckingham|George Villiers]], the first [[Duke of Buckingham]], is assassinated by [[John Felton (assassin)|John Felton]].
*[[1650]] &amp;ndash; Colonel [[George Monck, 1st Duke of Albemarle|George Monck]] of the [[English Army]] forms Monck's Regiment of Foot, which will later become the [[Coldstream Guards]].
*[[1655]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Sobota]]: The [[Swedish Empire]] led by [[Charles X Gustav]] defeats the [[Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth]].
*[[1703]] &amp;ndash; [[Edirne event]]: Sultan [[Mustafa II]] of the [[Ottoman Empire]] is dethroned.
*[[1775]] &amp;ndash; [[American Revolutionary War]]: [[George III of the United Kingdom|King George III]] delivers his [[Proclamation of Rebellion]] to the [[Court of St James's]] stating that the [[Thirteen Colonies|American colonies]] have proceeded to a state of open and avowed rebellion.
*[[1784]] &amp;ndash; Western [[North Carolina]] (now eastern [[Tennessee]]) declares itself an independent state under the name of [[State of Franklin|Franklin]]; it is not accepted into the United States, and only lasts for four years.
*[[1799]] &amp;ndash; [[Napoleon|Napoleon I of France]] leaves [[Egypt]] for France en route to seizing power.
*[[1813]] &amp;ndash; At the [[Battle of Großbeeren]], the [[Old Prussians|Prussians]] under [[Friedrich Wilhelm Freiherr von Bülow|Von Bülow]] repulse the French army.
*[[1831]] &amp;ndash; [[Nat Turner's slave rebellion]] is suppressed.
*[[1839]] &amp;ndash; The United Kingdom captures Hong Kong as a base as it prepares for war with [[Qing dynasty|Qing China]]. The ensuing three-year conflict will later be known as the [[First Opium War]].
*[[1864]] &amp;ndash; The [[Union Navy]] [[Siege of Fort Morgan|captures Fort Morgan, Alabama]], thus breaking [[Confederate States of America|Confederate]] dominance of all ports on the [[Gulf of Mexico]] except [[Galveston, Texas]].
*[[1866]] &amp;ndash; [[Austro-Prussian War]] ends with the [[Peace of Prague (1866)|Treaty of Prague]].
*[[1873]] &amp;ndash; [[Albert Bridge, London|Albert Bridge]] in [[Chelsea, London]] opens.
*[[1898]] &amp;ndash; The [[Southern Cross Expedition]], the first British venture of the [[Heroic Age of Antarctic Exploration]], departs from London.
*[[1904]] &amp;ndash; The [[tire chain|automobile tire chain]] is patented.
*[[1914]] &amp;ndash; [[World War I]]: [[Battle of Mons]]: The British Army begins withdrawal.
*[[1921]] &amp;ndash; British [[R38-class airship|airship R-38]] experiences structural failure over [[Kingston upon Hull|Hull]] in England and crashes in the [[Humber estuary]]. Of her 49 British and American training crew, only four survive.
*[[1923]] &amp;ndash; [[Captain (United States O-3)|Captain]] [[Lowell Smith]] and [[Lieutenant]] John P. Richter performed the first [[mid-air refueling]] on [[De Havilland DH-4B]], setting an endurance flight record of 37 hours.
*[[1927]] &amp;ndash; Italian anarchists [[Sacco and Vanzetti]] are executed after a lengthy, controversial trial.
*[[1929]] &amp;ndash; [[1929 Hebron massacre|Hebron Massacre]] during the [[1929 Palestine riots]]: [[Palestinian people|Arab]] attack on the Jewish community in [[Hebron]] in the [[British Mandate of Palestine]], continuing until the next day, resulted in the death of 65–68 Jews and the remaining Jews being forced to leave the city.
*[[1939]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: [[Nazi Germany]] and the [[Soviet Union]] sign a non-aggression treaty, the [[Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact]]. In a secret addition to the pact, the [[Baltic states]], Finland, [[Kingdom of Romania|Romania]], and [[Second Polish Republic|Poland]] are divided between the two nations.
*[[1942]] &amp;ndash; World War II: Beginning of the [[Battle of Stalingrad]].
*[[1943]] &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Kharkiv]] is liberated after the [[Battle of Kursk]].
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Marseille]] is [[Liberation of Marseille|liberated]] by the Allies.
*  1944   &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Michael of Romania|King Michael of Romania]] [[King Michael's Coup|dismisses]] the pro-[[Nazism|Nazi]] government of [[Ion Antonescu|Marshal Antonescu]], who is arrested. [[Romania]] switches sides from the [[Axis powers of World War II|Axis]] to the [[Allies of World War II|Allies]].
*  1944   &amp;ndash; [[Freckleton Air Disaster]]: A [[United States Army Air Forces]] [[B-24 Liberator]] bomber crashes into a school in [[Freckleton]], England, killing 61 people.
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; [[Soviet–Japanese War (1945)|Soviet–Japanese War]]: The [[USSR State Defense Committee]] issues Decree no. 9898cc &quot;About Receiving, Accommodation, and Labor Utilization of the [[Japanese prisoners of war in the Soviet Union|Japanese Army Prisoners of War]]&quot;.
*[[1946]] &amp;ndash; [[Ordinance No. 46]] of the British Military Government constitutes the German ''[[states of Germany|Länder]]'' (states) of [[State of Hanover|Hanover]] and [[Schleswig-Holstein]].
*[[1948]] &amp;ndash; [[World Council of Churches]] is formed by 147 churches from 44 countries.
*[[1954]] &amp;ndash; First flight of the [[Lockheed C-130]] multi-role aircraft.
*[[1958]] &amp;ndash; [[Chinese Civil War]]: The [[Second Taiwan Strait Crisis]] begins with the [[People's Liberation Army]]'s bombardment of [[Quemoy]].
*[[1966]] &amp;ndash; [[Lunar Orbiter 1]] takes the first photograph of Earth from orbit around the [[Moon]].
*[[1970]] &amp;ndash; Organized by [[Mexican American]] [[trade union|labor union]] leader [[César Chávez]], the [[Salad Bowl strike]], the largest farm worker [[strike action|strike]] in U.S. history, begins.
*[[1973]] &amp;ndash; A [[Norrmalmstorg robbery|bank robbery gone wrong]] in [[Stockholm]], Sweden, turns into a hostage crisis; over the next five days the hostages begin to sympathise with their captors, leading to the term &quot;[[Stockholm syndrome]]&quot;.
*[[1985]] &amp;ndash; [[Hans Tiedge]], top [[counter-spy]] of West Germany, defects to [[East Germany]].
*[[1987]] &amp;ndash; The American male basketball team lost the gold medal to Brazilian team at the [[Pan American Games]] in [[Indianapolis]], 120–115.
*[[1989]] &amp;ndash; [[Singing Revolution]]: Two million people from [[Estonia]], [[Latvia]] and [[Lithuania]] stand on the [[Vilnius]]–[[Tallinn]] road, [[Baltic Way|holding hands]].
*[[1990]] &amp;ndash; [[Saddam Hussein]] appears on [[Iraq]]i state television with a number of [[Western world|Western]] &quot;guests&quot; (actually hostages) to try to prevent the [[Gulf War]].
*  1990   &amp;ndash; [[Armenia]] declares its independence from the [[Soviet Union]].
*  1990   &amp;ndash; West and East Germany announce that they will reunite on [[October 3]].
*[[1991]] &amp;ndash; The [[World Wide Web]] is opened to the public.
*[[1994]] &amp;ndash; [[Eugene Bullard]], the only black pilot in World War I, is posthumously commissioned as [[Second Lieutenant]] in the [[United States Air Force]].
*[[2000]] &amp;ndash; [[Gulf Air Flight 072]] crashes into the [[Persian Gulf]] near [[Manama, Bahrain]], killing 143.
*[[2006]] &amp;ndash; [[Natascha Kampusch]], who had been abducted at the age of ten, escapes from her captor [[Wolfgang Přiklopil]], after eight years of captivity.
*[[2007]] &amp;ndash; The skeletal remains of Russia's last royal family members [[Alexei Nikolaevich, Tsarevich of Russia]], and his sister [[Grand Duchess Anastasia Nikolaevna of Russia|Grand Duchess Anastasia]] are discovered near [[Yekaterinburg]], Russia.
*[[2011]] &amp;ndash; A magnitude 5.8 (class: moderate) [[2011 Virginia earthquake|earthquake]] occurs in [[Virginia]]. Damage occurs to monuments and structures in Washington D.C. and the resulted damage is estimated at $200&amp;nbsp;million–$300&amp;nbsp;million USD.
*  2011   &amp;ndash; Libyan leader [[Muammar Gaddafi]] is overthrown after the [[National Transitional Council]] forces take control of [[Bab al-Azizia]] compound during the [[Libyan Civil War (2011)|Libyan Civil War]].
*[[2012]] &amp;ndash; A hot-air balloon [[2012 Ljubljana Marshes hot air balloon crash|crashes]] near the Slovenian capital of [[Ljubljana]], killing six people and injuring 28 others.
*[[2013]] &amp;ndash; A [[2013 Palmasola prison riot|riot]] at the [[Palmasola]] prison complex in [[Santa Cruz de la Sierra|Santa Cruz, Bolivia]] kills 31 people.
