<text xml:space="preserve">'''Amalric''' or '''Amalaric''' (also Americ, Almerich, Emeric, Emerick and other variations) is a personal name derived from the tribal name ''Amal'' (referring to the Gothic [[Amali]]) and ''ric'' ([[Gothic language|Gothic]] ''reiks'') meaning &quot;ruler, prince&quot;.

Equivalents in different languages include:
*[[French language|French]]: Amaury (surname/given name), Amalric (surname), Amaurich (surname), Maury (surname)
*[[German language|German]]: Amalrich, Emmerich

*[[Italian language|Italian]]: [[Amerigo (disambiguation)|Amerigo]], [[Arrigo]]
*[[Hungarian language|Hungarian]]: Imre
*[[Latin]]: Amalricus, Americus, Almericus, Emericus
*[[Greek language|Greek]]: Emerikos
*[[Polish language|Polish]]: Amalaryk, Amalryk, Emeryk
*[[Dutch language|Dutch]]: Emmerik, Amerik,
*[[Portuguese language|Portuguese]]: Amauri, Américo, América
*[[Spanish language|Spanish]]: Américo
*[[Serbian language|Serbo-Croatian]]: Mirko

==Given name==
* [[Amalaric]] (502–531), King of the Visigoths from 526 to 531
* [[Malaric]] (fl. 585), King of the Suevi
* [[Amalric of Nesle]] (fl. 1151–1180), Patriarch of Jerusalem from 1158 to 1180
* [[Amalric I of Jerusalem]] (1136–1174), King of Jerusalem from 1162 to 1174
* [[Amalric II of Jerusalem]] (fl. 1155–1205), King of Jerusalem from 1197 to 1205
* [[Amalric of Bena]] (f. 1200–1204), French theologian
* [[Arnaud Amalric]] (fl. 1196–1225), seventeenth abbot of Citeaux
* [[Amaury VI of Montfort]] (1195–1241), crusader
* [[Amalric, Lord of Tyre]] (c. 1272 – 1310), Governor of Cyprus from 1306 to 1310
* [[Amalric Gérard]] (born 1980), French actor

==Surname==
* [[Mathieu Amalric]] (born 1965), French actor and director
* [[Leonid Amalrik]] (1905–1997), Soviet animator
* [[Andrei Amalrik]] (1938–1980), Soviet dissident
