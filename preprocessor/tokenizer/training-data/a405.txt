==Demographics trends==
[[File:Pyramid armenia.jpg|thumb|600px|right|Pyramid Armenia 2012 &lt;br/&gt;http://www.armstat.am]]
After registering a steady increase all through the Soviet period, the [[Censuses of Armenia|population of Armenia]] declined from 3.604 million in 1991 to 3.211 million in 2003. The latest estimate is 2.872 million on October 12, 2011.&lt;ref name=&quot;В Армении опубликованы предварительные данные переписи населения&quot;&gt;News.am: [http://news.am/eng/news/92282.html Armenia’s population makes 3,285,767 – preliminary census results] – date of publication: February 07, 2012&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref name=&quot;Данные переписи населения Армении&quot;&gt;Газета &quot;Третья Сила&quot;—Thirdpower.am: [http://www.thirdpower.am/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=1348:2012-02-09-18-44-30&amp;catid=36:2009-11-14-13-03-47&amp;Itemid=1 Данные переписи населения Армении] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.is/20121206045614/http://www.thirdpower.am/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=1348:2012-02-09-18-44-30&amp;catid=36:2009-11-14-13-03-47&amp;Itemid=1 |date=2012-12-06 }}–дата публикации: 10.02.12&lt;/ref&gt;

[[Armenia]] is the only republic of the [[Post-Soviet states|former Soviet Union]] that has a nearly homogeneous population.  It is also the second-most densely populated post-Soviet state after [[Moldova]].  Ethnic minorities include [[Russians]], [[Assyrian people|Assyrians]], [[Ukrainians]], [[Yazidi]] [[Kurds]], [[Persian people|Iranians]], [[Greeks]], [[Georgians]], and [[Belarusians]].  There are also smaller communities of [[Vlachs]], [[Mordvin people|Mordvins]], [[Ossetians]], [[Udi people|Udis]], and [[Tat people (Caucasus)|Tats]]. Minorities of [[Poles]] and [[Caucasus Germans]] also exist though they are heavily [[Russification|Russified]].&lt;ref name=&quot;minorities&quot;&gt;Garnik Asatryan and Victoria Arakelova, ''[http://www.osce.org/documents/oy/2002/01/148_en.pdf The Ethnic Minorities of Armenia] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070808000000/http://www.osce.org/documents/oy/2002/01/148_en.pdf |date=August 8, 2007 }}'', Routledge, part of the [[Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe|OSCE]], 2002&lt;/ref&gt;

Most Armenians are Christian, primarily of [[Armenian Apostolic Church|Apostolic Church]] rite. Armenia is considered the first nation to adopt Christianity, which was first preached in Armenia by two Apostles of [[Jesus]], [[St. Bartholomew]] and [[St. Thaddeus]] in the 1st century.  The [[Armenian Apostolic Church]] can  trace its roots back to the 3rd and 4th centuries.  The country formally adopted the Christian faith in 301 A.D. Over 90% of Armenians belong to the Armenian Apostolic Church. It is a very ritualistic, conservative church, roughly comparable to the Coptic and Syrian churches. Armenia also has a population of [[Armenian Catholic Church|Catholics]] and evangelical Protestants.

The country's population has declined due to increased [[emigration]] since the break-up of the Soviet Union. The rates of emigration and population decline, however, have decreased in recent years, and there has been a moderate influx of Armenians returning to Armenia.

==Languages==
:[[Armenian language|Armenian]] 97.7%, [[Kurdish language|Kurdish]] 1%, [[Russian language|Russian]] 0.9%, and other 0.4% (2001 census).

[[Armenian language|Armenian]] is the only official language.

Armenia is a member of the [[La Francophonie]] due to a small percentage of people studying enough [[French language|French]].

The largest communities of the ethnic [[Armenian diaspora]] are fluent in [[Russian language|Russian]] and [[English language|English]].
