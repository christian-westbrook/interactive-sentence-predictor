==Events==
* [[769]] &amp;ndash; The [[Lateran Council (769)|Lateran Council]] condemned the [[Council of Hieria]] and [[anathema]]tized its [[Iconoclasm|iconoclastic]] rulings.
*[[1071]] &amp;ndash; [[Bari]], the last [[Byzantine Empire|Byzantine]] possession in southern Italy, [[siege of Bari|is surrendered]] to [[Robert Guiscard]].
*[[1395]] &amp;ndash; [[Timur]] defeats [[Tokhtamysh]] of the Golden Horde at the [[Battle of the Terek River]]. The Golden Horde capital city, [[Sarai (city)|Sarai]], is razed to the ground and Timur installs a puppet ruler on the throne.
*[[1450]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Formigny]]: Toward the end of the [[Hundred Years' War]], the French attack and nearly annihilate English forces, ending English domination in Northern France.
*[[1632]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Rain]]: [[Sweden|Swedes]] under [[Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden|Gustavus Adolphus]] defeat the [[Holy Roman Empire]] during the [[Thirty Years' War]].
*[[1642]] &amp;ndash; [[Irish Confederate Wars]]: A [[Confederate Ireland|Confederate Irish]] militia is routed in the [[Battle of Kilrush]] when it attempts to halt the progress of a [[Royalist]] Army.
*[[1715]] &amp;ndash; The [[Yamasee War#Pocotaligo massacre|Pocotaligo Massacre]] triggers the start of the [[Yamasee War]] in [[Province of Carolina|colonial South Carolina]].
*[[1736]] &amp;ndash; Foundation of the [[Kingdom of Corsica (1736)|Kingdom of Corsica]]
*[[1738]] &amp;ndash; ''[[Serse]]'', an [[Italian opera]] by [[George Frideric Handel]] receives its premiere performance in [[London|London, England]].
*[[1755]] &amp;ndash; [[Samuel Johnson]]'s ''[[A Dictionary of the English Language]]'' is published in London.
*[[1783]] &amp;ndash; Preliminary articles of peace ending the [[American Revolutionary War]] (or American War of Independence) are ratified.
*[[1817]] &amp;ndash; [[Thomas Hopkins Gallaudet]] and [[Laurent Clerc]] founded the [[American School for the Deaf]], the first American school for deaf students, in [[Hartford, Connecticut]].
*[[1861]] &amp;ndash; President [[Abraham Lincoln]] calls for [[President Lincoln's 75,000 Volunteers|75,000 Volunteers]] to quell the insurrection that soon became the [[American Civil War]].
*[[1865]] &amp;ndash; President [[Abraham Lincoln]] dies after being shot the previous evening by actor [[John Wilkes Booth]]. Vice President [[Andrew Johnson]] becomes President upon Lincoln's death.
*[[1892]] &amp;ndash; The [[General Electric|General Electric Company]] is formed.
*[[1896]] &amp;ndash; Closing ceremony of the [[1896 Summer Olympics|Games of the I Olympiad]] in [[Athens]], Greece.
*[[1900]] &amp;ndash; [[Philippine–American War]]: [[Filipino people|Filipino]] guerrillas launch a surprise attack on U.S. infantry and begin a [[Siege of Catubig|four-day siege]] of [[Catubig, Northern Samar|Catubig]], [[Philippines]].
*[[1907]] &amp;ndash; [[Triangle Fraternity]] is founded at the [[University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign]]
*[[1912]] &amp;ndash; The British passenger liner {{RMS|Titanic}} [[Sinking of the RMS Titanic|sinks]] in the [[Atlantic Ocean|North Atlantic]] at 2:20&amp;nbsp;a.m., two hours and forty minutes after hitting an [[iceberg]]. Only 710 of 2,227 passengers and crew on board survive.
*[[1920]] &amp;ndash; Two security guards are murdered during a robbery in [[South Braintree, Massachusetts]]. [[Anarchism|Anarchists]] [[Sacco and Vanzetti]] would be convicted of and executed for the crime, amid much controversy.
*[[1922]] &amp;ndash; U.S. Senator [[John B. Kendrick]] of [[Wyoming]] introduces a resolution calling for an investigation of a secret land deal, which leads to the discovery of the [[Teapot Dome scandal]].
*[[1923]] &amp;ndash; [[Insulin]] becomes generally available for use by people with [[diabetes mellitus|diabetes]].
*[[1924]] &amp;ndash; [[Rand McNally]] publishes its first [[road atlas]].
*[[1936]] &amp;ndash; First day of the [[1936–39 Arab revolt in Palestine|Arab revolt]] in [[Mandatory Palestine]].
*[[1941]] &amp;ndash; In the [[Belfast Blitz]], two-hundred bombers of the German [[Luftwaffe]] attack [[Belfast]], killing around one thousand people.
*[[1942]] &amp;ndash; The [[George Cross]] is awarded &quot;to the island fortress of [[Malta]]: Its people and defenders&quot; by King [[George VI]].
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; [[Bergen-Belsen concentration camp]] is liberated.
*[[1947]] &amp;ndash; [[Jackie Robinson]] debuts for the [[History of the Brooklyn Dodgers|Brooklyn Dodgers]], breaking [[Baseball color line|baseball's color line]].
*[[1955]] &amp;ndash; [[McDonald's]] restaurant dates its founding to the opening of a franchised restaurant by [[Ray Kroc]], in [[Des Plaines, Illinois]]
*[[1960]] &amp;ndash; At [[Shaw University]] in [[Raleigh, North Carolina]], [[Ella Baker]] leads a conference that results in the creation of the [[Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee]], one of the principal organizations of the [[Civil Rights Movement]] in the 1960s.
*[[1969]] &amp;ndash; The [[EC-121 shootdown incident]]: [[North Korea]] shoots down a [[United States Navy]] aircraft over the [[Sea of Japan]], killing all 31 on board.
*[[1970]] &amp;ndash; During the [[Cambodian Civil War]], massacres of the [[Vietnamese Cambodians|Vietnamese minority]] results in 800 bodies flowing down the [[Mekong]] river into [[South Vietnam]].
*[[1986]] &amp;ndash; The United States launches [[Operation El Dorado Canyon]], its bombing raids against [[History of Libya under Muammar Gaddafi#Great Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriya (1977–2012)|Libyan targets]] in response to a bombing in West Germany that killed two U.S. servicemen.
*[[1989]] &amp;ndash; [[Hillsborough disaster]]: A human crush occurs at [[Hillsborough Stadium]], home of [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.|Sheffield Wednesday]], in the FA Cup Semi-final, resulting in the deaths of 96 [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] fans.
*  1989   &amp;ndash; Upon [[Hu Yaobang]]'s death, the [[Tiananmen Square protests of 1989]] begin in China.
*[[2013]] &amp;ndash; [[Boston Marathon bombings|Two bombs explode]] near the finish line at the [[2013 Boston Marathon|Boston Marathon]] in [[Boston]], [[Massachusetts]], killing three people and injuring 264 others.
*[[2014]] &amp;ndash; In the [[2014 Bentiu massacre|worst massacre]] of the [[South Sudanese Civil War]], at least 200 civilians were gunned down after seeking refuge in houses of worship as well as hospitals.
