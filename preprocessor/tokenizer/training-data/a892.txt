==Events==
* [[190]] &amp;ndash; [[Dong Zhuo]] has his troops evacuate the capital [[Luoyang]] and burn it to the ground.
* [[475]] &amp;ndash; [[Byzantine Emperor]] [[Basiliscus]] issues a circular letter (''Enkyklikon'') to the bishops of his empire, supporting the [[Monophysitism|Monophysite]] [[christological]] position.
* [[537]] &amp;ndash; [[Siege of Rome (537–38)|Siege of Rome]]: The Byzantine general [[Belisarius]] receives his promised reinforcements, 1,600 cavalry, mostly of [[Huns|Hunnic]] or [[Slavs|Slavic]] origin and expert [[Archery|bowmen]]. He starts, despite shortages, raids against the [[Ostrogoths|Gothic]] camps and [[Vitiges]] is forced into a stalemate.
*[[1241]] &amp;ndash; [[Battle of Legnica|Battle of Liegnitz]]: [[Mongol Empire|Mongol]] forces defeat the Polish and German armies.
*[[1288]] &amp;ndash; [[Mongol invasions of Vietnam]]: [[Yuan dynasty|Yuan]] forces are defeated by [[Trần dynasty|Trần]] forces in the [[Battle of Bạch Đằng (1288)|Battle of Bach Dang]] in present-day northern Vietnam.
*[[1388]] &amp;ndash; Despite being outnumbered 16 to 1, forces of the [[Old Swiss Confederacy]] are victorious over the [[Archduchy of Austria]] in the [[Battle of Näfels]].
*[[1413]] &amp;ndash; [[Henry V of England|Henry V]] is crowned King of [[Kingdom of England|England]].
*[[1440]] &amp;ndash; [[Christopher of Bavaria]] is appointed King of [[Kalmar Union|Denmark]].
*[[1454]] &amp;ndash; The [[Treaty of Lodi]] is signed, establishing a balance of power among northern Italian city-states for almost 50 years.
*[[1511]] &amp;ndash; [[St John's College, Cambridge]], England, founded by [[Lady Margaret Beaufort]], receives its charter.
*[[1585]] &amp;ndash; The expedition organised by Sir [[Walter Raleigh]] departs England for [[Roanoke Island]] (now in [[North Carolina]]) to establish the [[Roanoke Colony]].
*[[1609]] &amp;ndash; [[Eighty Years' War]]: [[Spanish Empire|Spain]] and the [[Dutch Republic]] sign the [[Treaty of Antwerp (1609)|Treaty of Antwerp]] to initiate twelve years of truce.
*  1609   &amp;ndash; [[Philip III of Spain]] issues [[Expulsion of the Moriscos|the decree of the &quot;Expulsion of the Moriscos&quot;]].
*[[1682]] &amp;ndash; [[René-Robert Cavelier, Sieur de La Salle|Robert Cavelier de La Salle]] discovers the mouth of the [[Mississippi River]], claims it for France and names it [[Louisiana (New France)|Louisiana]].
*[[1782]] &amp;ndash; [[American War of Independence]]: [[Battle of the Saintes]] begins.
*[[1860]] &amp;ndash; On his [[phonautograph]] machine, [[Édouard-Léon Scott de Martinville]] makes the [[history of sound recording|oldest known recording]] of an audible human voice.
*[[1865]] &amp;ndash; [[American Civil War]]: [[Robert E. Lee]] surrenders the [[Army of Northern Virginia]] (26,765 troops) to [[Ulysses S. Grant]] at [[Appomattox Courthouse, Virginia]], effectively ending the war.
*[[1909]] &amp;ndash; The [[United States Congress|U.S. Congress]] passes the [[Payne–Aldrich Tariff Act]].
*[[1914]] &amp;ndash; [[Mexican Revolution]]: One of the world's first [[Action of 9 April 1914|naval/air skirmishes]] takes place off the coast of western Mexico.
*[[1916]] &amp;ndash; [[World War I]]: The [[Battle of Verdun]]: German forces launch their third offensive of the battle.
*[[1917]] &amp;ndash; World War I: The [[Battle of Arras (1917)|Battle of Arras]]: The battle begins with [[Canadian Corps]] executing a massive assault on [[Vimy Ridge]].
*[[1918]] &amp;ndash; World War I: The [[Battle of the Lys (1918)|Battle of the Lys]]: The [[Portuguese Expeditionary Corps]] is crushed by the German forces during what is called the [[Spring Offensive]] on the Belgian region of [[Flanders]].
*  1918   &amp;ndash; The National Council of [[Bessarabia]] proclaims [[Union of Bessarabia with Romania|union]] with the [[Kingdom of Romania]].
*[[1937]] &amp;ndash; The ''[[Kamikaze (1937 aircraft)|Kamikaze]]'' arrives at [[Croydon Airport]] in London. It is the first Japanese-built aircraft to fly to Europe.
*[[1939]] &amp;ndash; [[Marian Anderson]] sings at the [[Lincoln Memorial]], after being denied the right to sing at the [[Daughters of the American Revolution]]'s [[Constitution Hall]].
*[[1940]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: [[Operation Weserübung]]: [[Nazi Germany|Germany]] invades Denmark and Norway.
*  1940   &amp;ndash; [[Vidkun Quisling]] seizes power in Norway.
*[[1942]] &amp;ndash; World War II: The [[Battle of Bataan]]/[[Bataan Death March]]: United States forces surrender on the [[Bataan Peninsula]]. The [[Imperial Japanese Navy|Japanese Navy]] launches an air raid on [[Trincomalee]] in [[Ceylon]] ([[Sri Lanka]]); [[Royal Navy]] [[aircraft carrier]] {{HMS|Hermes|95|6}} and [[Royal Australian Navy]] [[Destroyer]] {{HMAS|Vampire|D68|6}} are sunk off the island's east coast.
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; Execution of [[Dietrich Bonhoeffer]], anti-Nazi dissident and spy, by the Nazi regime.
* 1945    &amp;ndash; World War II: The German pocket battleship [[German cruiser Admiral Scheer|''Admiral Scheer'']] is sunk by the [[Royal Air Force]]
*  1945   &amp;ndash; World War II: The [[Battle of Königsberg]], in [[East Prussia]], ends.
*  1945   &amp;ndash; The [[United States Atomic Energy Commission]] is formed.
*[[1947]] &amp;ndash; The [[Glazier–Higgins–Woodward tornadoes]] kill 181 and injure 970 in [[Texas]], [[Oklahoma]], and [[Kansas]].
*  1947   &amp;ndash; The [[Journey of Reconciliation]], the first interracial [[Freedom Ride]] begins through the upper [[Southern United States|South]] in violation of [[Jim Crow laws]]. The riders wanted enforcement of the [[United States Supreme Court]]'s [[1946]] [[Irene Morgan]] decision that banned [[racial segregation]] in interstate travel.
*[[1948]] &amp;ndash; [[Jorge Eliécer Gaitán]]'s assassination provokes a violent riot in [[Bogotá]] (the ''[[Bogotazo]]''), and a further ten years of violence in [[Colombia]].
*  1948   &amp;ndash; Fighters from the [[Irgun]] and [[Lehi (group)|Lehi]] [[Zionist]] paramilitary groups attacked [[Deir Yassin]] near [[Jerusalem]], [[Deir Yassin massacre|killing over 100]].
*[[1952]] &amp;ndash; [[Hugo Ballivián]]'s government is overthrown by the [[History of Bolivia (1920–64)|Bolivian National Revolution]], starting a period of [[agrarian reform]], [[universal suffrage]] and the [[nationalization]] of tin mines
*[[1957]] &amp;ndash; The [[Suez Canal]] in [[Egypt]] is cleared and opens to shipping.
*[[1959]] &amp;ndash; [[Project Mercury]]: [[NASA]] announces the selection of the United States' first seven [[astronaut]]s, whom the news media quickly dub the &quot;[[Mercury Seven]]&quot;.
*[[1960]] &amp;ndash; Dr [[Hendrik Verwoerd]], Prime Minister of South Africa and architect of [[apartheid]], narrowly survives an assassination attempt by a white farmer, [[David Pratt (assassin)|David Pratt]] in [[Johannesburg]].
*[[1961]] &amp;ndash; The [[Pacific Electric Railway]] in Los Angeles, once the largest electric railway in the world, ends operations.
*[[1965]] &amp;ndash; [[Astrodome]] opens. First indoor [[baseball]] game is played.
*[[1967]] &amp;ndash; The first [[Boeing 737]] (a 100 series) makes its maiden flight.
*[[1969]] &amp;ndash; The first British-built ''[[Concorde]]'' 002 makes its maiden flight from [[Filton]] to [[RAF Fairford]].
*[[1975]] &amp;ndash; The first game of the [[Philippine Basketball Association]], the second oldest professional basketball league in the world.
*[[1976]] &amp;ndash; The [[EMD F40PH]] diesel locomotive enters revenue service with [[Amtrak]].
*[[1980]] &amp;ndash; The [[Iraq]]i regime of [[Saddam Hussein]] kills philosopher [[Muhammad Baqir al-Sadr]] and his sister [[Bint al-Huda]] after three days of torture.
*[[1981]] &amp;ndash; The U.S. Navy nuclear submarine {{USS|George Washington|SSBN-598|6}} accidentally collides with the ''Nissho Maru'', a Japanese cargo ship, sinking it.&lt;!-- any fatalities? --&gt;
*[[1989]] &amp;ndash; [[April 9 tragedy|Tbilisi massacre]]: an [[Anti-Sovietism|anti-Soviet]] peaceful demonstration and hunger strike in [[Tbilisi]], demanding restoration of Georgian independence, is [[April 9 tragedy|dispersed by the Soviet Army]], resulting in 20 deaths and hundreds of injuries.
*[[1991]] &amp;ndash; [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]] declares independence from the [[Soviet Union]]
*[[1992]] &amp;ndash; A [[United States federal courts|U.S. Federal Court]] finds former [[Panama]]nian dictator [[Manuel Noriega]] guilty of drug and racketeering charges. He is sentenced to 30 years in prison.
*[[1999]] &amp;ndash; [[Kosovo War]]: The [[Battle of Košare]] begins.
*[[2003]] &amp;ndash; [[Iraq War]]: [[Baghdad]] falls to American forces; [[Iraqis]] turn on symbols of their former leader [[Saddam Hussein]], pulling down a grand statue of him and tearing it to pieces.
*[[2005]] &amp;ndash; [[Charles, Prince of Wales]] [[Wedding of Charles, Prince of Wales, and Camilla Parker Bowles|marries]] [[Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall|Camilla Parker Bowles]] in a civil ceremony at [[Windsor, Berkshire|Windsor]]'s Guildhall.
*[[2009]] &amp;ndash; In [[Tbilisi]], [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]], up to 60,000 people [[2009 Georgian demonstrations|protest]] against the government of [[Mikheil Saakashvili]].
*[[2013]] &amp;ndash; A 6.1–magnitude [[2013 Bushehr earthquake|earthquake]] strikes [[Iran]] killing 32 people and injuring over 850 people.
*  2013   &amp;ndash; At least 13 people are killed and another three injured after a man goes on a [[Velika Ivanča shooting|spree shooting]] in the Serbian village of [[Velika Ivanča]].
*[[2014]] &amp;ndash; A student [[Franklin Regional High School stabbing|stabs]] 20 people at [[Franklin Regional High School]] in [[Murrysville, Pennsylvania]].
*[[2017]] &amp;ndash; [[2017 Palm Sunday church bombings|Palm Sunday church bombings]] at Coptic Churches in [[Tanta]] and [[Alexandria]] take place.
