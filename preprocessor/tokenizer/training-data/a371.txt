==Events==
*[[48 BC]] &amp;ndash; [[Caesar's Civil War]]: [[Battle of Pharsalus]]: [[Julius Caesar]] decisively defeats [[Pompey]] at [[Pharsalus]] and Pompey flees to [[Ancient Egypt|Egypt]].
* [[378]] &amp;ndash; [[Gothic War (376–382)|Gothic War]]: [[Battle of Adrianople]]: A large [[Roman Empire|Roman]] army led by Emperor [[Valens]] is defeated by the [[Visigoths]]. Valens is killed along with over half of his army.
*[[1173]] &amp;ndash; Construction of the [[campanile]] of the [[Cathedral of Pisa]] (now known as the [[Leaning Tower of Pisa]]) begins; it will take two centuries to complete.
*[[1329]] &amp;ndash; [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Quilon|Quilon]], the first [[Christianity in India|Indian Christian Diocese]], is erected by [[Pope John XXII]]; the French-born [[Jordanus]] is appointed the first Bishop.
*[[1500]] &amp;ndash; [[Ottoman–Venetian War (1499–1503)]]: The Ottomans capture [[Methoni, Messenia]].
*[[1610]] &amp;ndash; The [[Anglo-Powhatan Wars|First Anglo-Powhatan War]] begins in [[Colony of Virginia|colonial Virginia]].
*[[1810]] &amp;ndash; [[Napoleon]] annexes [[Westphalia]] as part of the [[First French Empire]].
*[[1814]] &amp;ndash; [[Indian Wars]]: The [[Creek people|Creek]] sign the [[Treaty of Fort Jackson]], giving up huge parts of [[Alabama]] and [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]].
*[[1830]] &amp;ndash; [[Louis Philippe]] becomes the king of the French following abdication of Charles X.
*[[1842]] &amp;ndash; The [[Webster–Ashburton Treaty]] is signed, establishing the United States–Canada border east of the [[Rocky Mountains]].
*[[1854]] &amp;ndash; [[Henry David Thoreau]] publishes ''[[Walden]]''.
*[[1862]] &amp;ndash; [[American Civil War]]: [[Battle of Cedar Mountain]]: At [[Culpeper County, Virginia|Cedar Mountain, Virginia]], [[Confederate States of America|Confederate]] General [[Stonewall Jackson]] narrowly defeats [[Union Army|Union]] forces under General [[John Pope (military officer)|John Pope]].
*[[1877]] &amp;ndash; [[Indian Wars]]: [[Battle of the Big Hole]]: A small band of [[Nez Perce people|Nez Percé Indians]] clash with the [[United States Army]]
*[[1892]] &amp;ndash; [[Thomas Edison]] receives a [[patent]] for a two-way [[Telegraphy|telegraph]].
*[[1896]] &amp;ndash; Glider pioneer [[Otto Lilienthal]] dies in a [[Otto_Lilienthal#Final_flight|fatal crash]].
*[[1902]] &amp;ndash; [[Edward VII]] and [[Alexandra of Denmark]] are crowned King and Queen of the [[United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland]].
*[[1907]] &amp;ndash; The first [[Boy Scout]] [[Brownsea Island Scout camp|encampment]] concludes at [[Brownsea Island]] in southern England.
*[[1914]] &amp;ndash; Start of the [[Battle of Mulhouse]], part of a French attempt to recover the province of [[Alsace]] and the first French offensive of [[World War I]].
*[[1925]] &amp;ndash; A [[Kakori train robbery|train robbery]] takes place in [[Kakori]], near [[Lucknow]], India
*[[1930]] &amp;ndash; [[Betty Boop]] makes her cartoon debut in ''[[Dizzy Dishes]]''.
*[[1936]] &amp;ndash; [[Summer Olympic Games]]: [[1936 Summer Olympics|Games of the XI Olympiad]]: [[Jesse Owens]] wins his fourth [[gold medal]] at the games.
*[[1942]] &amp;ndash; [[World War II]]: [[Battle of Savo Island]]: [[Allies of World War II|Allied]] naval forces protecting their amphibious forces during the initial stages of the [[Battle of Guadalcanal]] are surprised and defeated by an [[Imperial Japanese Navy]] cruiser force.
*[[1944]] &amp;ndash; The [[United States Forest Service]] and the Wartime Advertising Council release posters featuring [[Smokey Bear]] for the first time.
*  1944   &amp;ndash; [[Continuation War]]: The [[Vyborg–Petrozavodsk Offensive]], the largest offensive launched by Soviet Union against Finland during the Second World War, ends to a strategic stalemate. Both Finnish and Soviet troops at the Finnish front dug to defensive positions, and the front remains stable until the end of the war.
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Nagasaki]] is [[Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki|devastated]] when an [[nuclear weapon|atomic bomb]], ''[[Fat Man]]'', is dropped by the United States [[B-29 Superfortress|B-29]] ''[[Bockscar]]''. Thirty-five thousand people are killed outright, including 23,200-28,200 Japanese war workers, 2,000 Korean forced workers, and 150 Japanese soldiers.
*  1945   &amp;ndash; The [[Red Army]] [[Soviet invasion of Manchuria|invades]] Japanese-occupied Manchuria.
*[[1960]] &amp;ndash; [[South Kasai]] secedes from the [[Republic of the Congo (Léopoldville)|Congo]].
*[[1965]] &amp;ndash; [[Singapore]] is [[History of the Republic of Singapore#Independence of Singapore|expelled]] from [[Malaysia]] and becomes the only country to date to gain independence unwillingly.
*[[1969]] &amp;ndash; Followers of [[Charles Manson]] murder pregnant actress [[Sharon Tate]] (wife of [[Roman Polanski]]), [[coffee]] heiress [[Abigail Folger]], Polish actor [[Wojciech Frykowski]], men's hairstylist [[Jay Sebring]] and recent high-school graduate [[Steven Parent]].
*[[1971]] &amp;ndash; [[The Troubles]]: The British Army in [[Northern Ireland]] launches [[Operation Demetrius]]. Hundreds of people are arrested and [[Internment|interned]], thousands are displaced, and twenty are killed in the violence that followed.
*[[1973]] &amp;ndash; [[Mars 7]] is launched from the USSR.
*[[1974]] &amp;ndash; As a direct result of the [[Watergate scandal]], [[Richard Nixon]] becomes the first President of the United States to resign from office. His [[Vice President of the United States of America|Vice President]], [[Gerald Ford]], becomes president.
*[[1991]] &amp;ndash; The Italian prosecuting magistrate [[Antonino Scopelliti]] is murdered by the [['Ndrangheta]] on behalf of the [[Sicilian Mafia]] while preparing the government’s case in the final appeal of the [[Maxi Trial]].
*[[1993]] &amp;ndash; The [[Liberal Democratic Party (Japan)|Liberal Democratic Party of Japan]] loses a 38-year hold on national leadership.
*[[1999]] &amp;ndash; Russian President [[Boris Yeltsin]] fires his Prime Minister, [[Sergei Stepashin]], and for the fourth time fires his entire cabinet.
*[[2006]] &amp;ndash; At least 21 suspected terrorists were arrested in the [[2006 transatlantic aircraft plot]] that happened in the United Kingdom. The arrests were made in London, [[Birmingham]], and [[High Wycombe]] in an overnight operation.
*[[2013]] &amp;ndash; Gunmen [[9 August 2013 Quetta shooting|open fire]] at a [[Sunni]] mosque in the city of [[Quetta]] killing at least ten people and injuring 30.
*[[2014]] &amp;ndash; Michael Brown, an 18-year-old African American male in [[Ferguson, Missouri]], was [[Shooting of Michael Brown|shot and killed]] by a [[Ferguson Police Department (Missouri)|Ferguson]] police officer after reportedly assaulting the officer and attempting to steal his weapon, sparking [[Ferguson unrest|protests and unrest]] in the city.
