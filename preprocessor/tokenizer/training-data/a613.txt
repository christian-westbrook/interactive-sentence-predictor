==Events==
*[[29 BC]] &amp;ndash; [[Octavian]] holds the second of three consecutive [[Roman triumph|triumphs]] in [[Ancient Rome|Rome]] to celebrate the victory over the [[Dalmatian tribes]].
*[[1040]] &amp;ndash; King [[Duncan I of Scotland|Duncan I]] is killed in battle against his first cousin and rival [[Macbeth, King of Scotland|Macbeth]]. The latter succeeds him as [[List of Scottish monarchs|King of Scotland]].
*[[1183]] &amp;ndash; [[Taira no Munemori]] and the [[Taira clan]] take the young [[Emperor Antoku]] and the [[Imperial Regalia of Japan|three sacred treasures]] and flee to western Japan to escape pursuit by the [[Minamoto clan]] (traditional [[Japanese calendar|Japanese date]]: Twenty-fifth Day of the Seventh Month of the Second Year of [[Juei]]).
*[[1288]] &amp;ndash; Count [[Adolf VIII of Berg]] grants [[town privileges]] to [[Düsseldorf]], the village on the banks of the [[Düssel]].
*[[1352]] &amp;ndash; [[War of the Breton Succession]]: Anglo-Bretons defeat the [[Kingdom of France|French]] in the [[Battle of Mauron]].
*[[1370]] &amp;ndash; [[Charles IV, Holy Roman Emperor]], grants [[city privileges]] to [[Karlovy Vary|Carlsbad]] which is subsequently named after him.
*[[1385]] &amp;ndash; [[1383–85 Portuguese interregnum|Portuguese Crisis of 1383–85]]: [[Battle of Aljubarrota]]: Portuguese forces commanded by [[John I of Portugal|King John I]] and his [[General officer|general]] [[Nuno Álvares Pereira]] defeat the [[Castile (historical region)|Castilian]] army of [[John I of Castile|King John I]].
*[[1457]] &amp;ndash; Publication of the [[Mainz Psalter]], the first book to feature a printed date of publication and printed colophon
*[[1480]] &amp;ndash; [[Ottoman invasion of Otranto|Battle of Otranto]]: [[Ottoman Empire|Ottoman]] troops behead 800 [[Christian]]s for refusing to convert to [[Islam]]; they are later [[Martyrs of Otranto|honored]] in the Church.
*[[1592]] &amp;ndash; The first sighting of the [[Falkland Islands]] by [[John Davis (English explorer)|John Davis]].
*[[1598]] &amp;ndash; [[Nine Years' War (Ireland)|Nine Years' War]]: [[Battle of the Yellow Ford]]: Irish forces under [[Hugh O'Neill, Earl of Tyrone]], defeats an English expeditionary force under [[Henry Bagenal]].
*[[1720]] &amp;ndash; The Spanish military [[Villasur expedition]] is wiped out by [[Pawnee people|Pawnee]] and [[Otoe tribe|Otoe]] warriors near present-day [[Columbus, Nebraska]].
*[[1791]] &amp;ndash; Slaves from plantations in [[Saint-Domingue]] hold a [[Haitian Vodou|Vodou]] ceremony lead by [[houngan]] [[Dutty Boukman]] at [[Bois Caïman]], marking the start of the [[Haitian Revolution]].
*[[1814]] &amp;ndash;  A cease fire agreement, called the [[Convention of Moss]], ended the [[Swedish–Norwegian War (1814)|Swedish–Norwegian War]].
*[[1816]] &amp;ndash; The United Kingdom formally annexes the [[Tristan da Cunha]] archipelago, administering the islands from the [[Cape Colony]] in South Africa.
*[[1842]] &amp;ndash; [[American Indian Wars]]: [[Seminole Wars|Second Seminole War]] ends, with the [[Seminole]]s forced from [[Florida]] to [[Oklahoma]].
*[[1848]] &amp;ndash; [[Oregon Territory]] is organized by [[act of Congress]].
*[[1880]] &amp;ndash; Construction of [[Cologne Cathedral]], the most famous landmark in [[Cologne]], Germany, is completed.
*[[1885]] &amp;ndash; [[Japanese patent law|Japan's first patent]] is issued to the inventor of a rust-proof paint.
*[[1888]] &amp;ndash; An [[Sound recording and reproduction|audio recording]] of English composer [[Arthur Sullivan]]'s &quot;[[The Lost Chord]]&quot;, one of the first recordings of music ever made, is played during a press conference introducing [[Thomas Edison]]'s [[phonograph]] in London, England.
*[[1893]] &amp;ndash; France becomes the first country to introduce [[vehicle registration plate|motor vehicle registration]].
*[[1900]] &amp;ndash; The [[Eight-Nation Alliance]] occupies Beijing, China, in a campaign to end the bloody [[Boxer Rebellion]] in China.
*[[1901]] &amp;ndash; The first claimed [[aviation|powered flight]], by [[Gustave Whitehead]] in his [[Whitehead No. 21|Number 21]].
*[[1911]] &amp;ndash; [[United States Senate]] leaders [[Presidents pro tempore of the United States Senate, 1911–1913|agree to rotate the office]] of [[President pro tempore of the United States Senate|President pro tempore of the Senate]] among leading candidates to fill the vacancy left by [[William P. Frye]]'s death.
*[[1912]] &amp;ndash; [[United States Marine Corps|U.S. Marines]] invade [[Nicaragua]] to support the U.S.-backed government installed there after [[José Santos Zelaya]] had resigned three years earlier.
*[[1914]] &amp;ndash; [[World War I]]: Start of the [[Battle of Lorraine]], an unsuccessful French offensive designed to recover the lost province of [[Moselle]] from Germany.
*[[1916]] &amp;ndash; [[Romania during World War I|Romania declares war on Austria-Hungary]].
*[[1921]] &amp;ndash; [[Tannu Uriankhai]], later [[Tuvan People's Republic]] is established as a completely independent country (which is supported by [[Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic|Soviet Russia]]).
*[[1933]] &amp;ndash; Loggers cause a [[Wildfire|forest fire]] in the [[Pacific Coast Ranges|Coast Range]] of [[Oregon]], later known as the first forest fire of the [[Tillamook Burn]]; it is not fully extinguished until September 5, after destroying {{convert|240,000|acre|km2}}.
*[[1935]] &amp;ndash; [[Franklin D. Roosevelt]] signs the [[Social Security Act]], creating a government pension system for the retired.
*[[1936]] &amp;ndash; [[Rainey Bethea]] is hanged in [[Owensboro, Kentucky]] in the last known public [[capital punishment in the United States|execution in the United States]].
*[[1937]] &amp;ndash; The beginning of [[Air combat manoeuvring|air-to-air combat]] of the [[Second Sino-Japanese War]] and [[World War II]] in general, when six Japanese bombers [[Aerial engagements of the Second Sino-Japanese War|are shot down by Chinese fighters]] while raiding Chinese air bases.
*[[1941]] &amp;ndash; World War II: [[Winston Churchill]] and [[Franklin D. Roosevelt]] sign the [[Atlantic Charter]] of war stating postwar aims.
*[[1945]] &amp;ndash; Japan accepts the Allied [[Japanese Instrument of Surrender|terms of surrender]] in World War II and the [[Emperor of Japan|Emperor]] records the [[Gyokuon-hōsō|Imperial Rescript on Surrender]] ([[August 15]] in [[Japan Standard Time]]).
*  1945   &amp;ndash; The [[Viet Minh]] launches [[August Revolution]] amid the political confusion and power vacuum engulfing [[Vietnam]].
*[[1947]] &amp;ndash; [[Pakistan]] gains [[Pakistan Movement|Independence]] from the [[British Empire]] and joins the [[Commonwealth of Nations]].
*[[1959]] &amp;ndash; Founding and first official meeting of the [[American Football League]].
*[[1967]] &amp;ndash; UK [[Marine, &amp;c., Broadcasting (Offences) Act 1967|Marine Broadcasting Offences Act]] declares participation in offshore [[pirate radio]] illegal.
*[[1969]] &amp;ndash; [[Operation Banner]]: British troops are deployed in [[Northern Ireland]].
*[[1971]] &amp;ndash; [[Bahrain]] declares independence as the State of Bahrain.
*[[1972]] &amp;ndash; An Ilyushin Il-62 airliner [[1972 Königs Wusterhausen air disaster|crashes near Königs Wusterhausen]], East Germany, due to an in-flight fire, killing 156.
*[[1973]] &amp;ndash; The [[Constitution of Pakistan|Pakistan Constitution of 1973]] comes into effect.
*[[1975]] &amp;ndash; ''[[The Rocky Horror Picture Show]]'', the longest-running release in film history, opens in London.
*[[1980]] &amp;ndash; [[Lech Wałęsa]] leads strikes at the [[Gdańsk]], Poland shipyards.
*[[1994]] &amp;ndash; Ilich Ramírez Sánchez, also known as &quot;[[Carlos the Jackal]]&quot;, is captured.
*[[1996]] &amp;ndash; Greek Cypriot refugee [[Solomos Solomou]] is murdered by Turkish forces while trying to climb a flagpole in order to remove a Turkish flag from its mast in the [[United Nations Buffer Zone in Cyprus]].
*[[2003]] &amp;ndash; A [[Northeast blackout of 2003|widescale power blackout]] affects the northeast United States and Canada.
*[[2003]]&amp;ndash; [[Project Thread]], an operation launched by CSIS and other Canadian law enforcement agencies, saw the arrest and incarceration of 24 innocent Muslim men, most of them young Pakistani students.
*[[2005]] &amp;ndash; [[Helios Airways Flight 522]], en route from Larnaca, [[Cyprus]] to Prague, Czech Republic via Athens, crashes in the hills near [[Grammatiko]], Greece, killing 121 passengers and crew.
*[[2006]] &amp;ndash; Sixty-one schoolgirls killed in [[Chencholai bombing]] by [[Sri Lanka Air force|Sri Lankan Air Force]] air strike.
*[[2007]] &amp;ndash; The [[2007 Yazidi communities bombings|Kahtaniya bombings]] kills at least 334 people.
*[[2013]] &amp;ndash; [[Egypt]] declares a [[state of emergency]] as security forces [[August 2013 Rabaa massacre|kill hundreds of demonstrators]] supporting former president [[Mohamed Morsi]].
*[[2015]] &amp;ndash; The [[Embassy of the United States, Havana|US Embassy]] in [[Havana, Cuba]] re-opens after 54 years of being closed when [[Cuba–United States relations]] were broken off.
